1. INTRODUCTION
================================================================================

MindFlex is a comprehensive web application designed to support individuals 
with ADHD in managing daily tasks through structured activities, relaxation 
tools, progress tracking, and personalized support strategies.

Key Features:
- Daily task management with morning, afternoon, and evening categories
- Brain games to improve focus and attention
- Yoga and wellness content for relaxation
- Music player with calming playlists
- Problem-based support system with personalized solutions
- Mood tracking with visual feedback
- Points and achievement system for motivation
- Face analysis for detecting fatigue and stress

Design Philosophy:
- Calming color palette (soft blues, greens, purples)
- Dyslexia-friendly fonts for improved readability
- Rounded, friendly interface elements
- Mobile-responsive design

8. SUPPORT SYSTEM
================================================================================

The Support section provides personalized strategies for common ADHD challenges.

How It Works:
1. View the list of 8 common ADHD challenges
2. Select one or more challenges you're currently facing
3. Click "Get Solutions" to receive personalized strategies
4. Review the tailored tips and techniques
5. Select new challenges anytime for additional support

Available Challenges & Solutions:

CHALLENGE 1: Easily distracted, difficulty finishing tasks
Solutions:
- Use timers (Pomodoro technique: 25 min focus + 5 min break)
- Break tasks into smaller steps
- Remove distractions (quiet workspace, phone limits)

CHALLENGE 2: Acting without thinking, interrupting, risky decisions
Solutions:
- Practice pause techniques (count to 5 before responding)
- Use role-play games like Simon Says to train impulse control
- Keep a journal to reflect before acting

CHALLENGE 3: Constant movement, difficulty sitting still
Solutions:
- Channel energy into exercise (running, yoga, dance)
- Use fidget tools (stress balls, doodling)
- Schedule short movement breaks during study/work

CHALLENGE 4: Anger, stress, mood swings
Solutions:
- Breathing exercises (inhale 4, exhale 6)
- Yoga poses like Child's Pose or Tree Pose
- Talk to a trusted friend or mentor when emotions rise

CHALLENGE 5: Poor organization, procrastination, forgetfulness
Solutions:
- Use planners, to-do lists, or digital apps
- Color-code tasks for priority
- Set reminders for deadlines and routines

CHALLENGE 6: Missed deadlines, inconsistent performance
Solutions:
- Study in short, focused sessions
- Ask for clear instructions and feedback
- Use visual aids (charts, diagrams) to understand better

CHALLENGE 7: Interrupting, forgetting commitments, miscommunication
Solutions:
- Practice active listening (repeat back what you heard)
- Use reminders for birthdays, meetings, promises
- Be open about ADHD with close friends/family for understanding

CHALLENGE 8: Feeling inadequate, anxiety, depression
Solutions:
- Celebrate small wins daily
- Keep a gratitude journal
- Seek ADHD coaching or professional support
