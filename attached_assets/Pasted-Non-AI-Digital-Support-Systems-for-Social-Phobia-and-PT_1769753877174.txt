Problem Statement ID	MENTIS0012
Problem Statement Title	Non-AI Technical Systems for Learning Support in ADHD & ASD
Description	
Problem Statement
Children with ADHD and Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) often face challenges related to attention, focus, organization, social understanding, and adaptive learning. These difficulties can hinder academic progress and classroom participation. While individualized teaching approaches and therapies are beneficial, there is a need for scalable, structured, and consistent non-AI tools that support daily learning and personal development in school and home environments.

This challenge invites participants to design non-AI technical systems that facilitate focus, engagement, adaptive learning, and skill-building for children with ADHD and ASD, without attempting clinical diagnosis or therapeutic intervention.

Background / Context
Students with ADHD may struggle with inattention, hyperactivity, and impulsivity, while students with ASD may experience challenges related to social interaction, communication, and executive functioning. Most classroom environments are not optimized for these learning differences, and educators often lack structured tools to consistently support individual needs.

Non-AI systems such as structured activity planners, visual schedules, interactive learning aids, and progress tracking dashboards can improve focus, engagement, and learning outcomes. These tools can also empower teachers, parents, and caregivers to monitor progress and coordinate support effectively.

Expected Technical Solutions
Structured learning and routine management tools including visual schedules, timers, and task segmentation applications
Focus and engagement support systems providing step-by-step guidance, break reminders, or simple gamified learning modules
Behavior and progress tracking platforms for recording observations, learning milestones, attention patterns, and adaptive skill development
Interactive educational aids such as non-AI quizzes, puzzles, or simulation-based learning activities suited to ADHD/ASD learning styles
Parent–teacher coordination tools enabling secure sharing of progress updates, feedback, and learning strategies
Privacy-first and child-safe architectures with consent-based access, minimal data collection, and secure storage compliant with child protection norms
Note: Solutions must support learning, focus, and engagement only and must not attempt diagnosis or clinical intervention.
